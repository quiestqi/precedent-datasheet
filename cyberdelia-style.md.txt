# Cyberdelia Website Style Guide

## Aesthetic

This website is inspired by Cyberdelia, a visual culture that combines early cyberculture, psychedelic imagery, and experimental digital aesthetics.

Instead of reproducing a nostalgic 1990s website, I wanted to reinterpret Cyberdelia as a contemporary exhibition interface.

## Color

The color palette is inspired by iridescent lighting and holographic surfaces.

- Deep indigo
- Lavender
- Ice blue
- Pearl white

These colors create an immersive atmosphere while maintaining readability.

## Typography

The website uses Exo 2 because it feels futuristic and clean without becoming overly mechanical.

Large headings establish hierarchy while lighter body text improves readability.

## Composition

I redesigned the original datasheet into an editorial layout.

Artist's Other Works, Exhibitions, and Awards are presented as vertical information blocks rather than traditional tables.

This creates a rhythm that resembles exhibition catalogues and contemporary design websites.

## Material

The interface uses glassmorphism, soft glows, and translucent layers to create depth.

A background image combined with blur effects reinforces the Cyberdelia atmosphere.

## AI-assisted Coding

I used ChatGPT as a design collaborator throughout the project.

Rather than generating the final website in a single step, I iteratively refined the visual language through multiple rounds of prompts, focusing on typography, color, composition, and interface details.
